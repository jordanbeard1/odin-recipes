Etouffee

Description

Etouffee is a classic Cajun dish made with a rich, flavorful sauce and a variety of meats or seafood, typically served over rice. The name "etouffee" means "smothered" in French, referring to the cooking method used to create the dish.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 cups seafood broth
  • 2 cups okra, sliced
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked white rice for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the flour and cook, stirring constantly, until it turns a deep brown color (about 20-30 minutes).
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic. Cook until softened.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook until pink.
  4. Add the broth, okra, Cajun seasoning, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes.
  6. Serve over cooked white rice.
